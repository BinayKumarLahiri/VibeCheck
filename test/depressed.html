Depressed

A thick, grey fog of hopelessness had settled over his mind for weeks, leeching all the color and meaning out of his daily routine. He was profoundly depressed, experiencing an all-encompassing fatigue that made even the simplest tasks feel monumental and pointless. The once-loved hobbies lay untouched, a symbol of the profound anhedonia—the sustained loss of pleasure—that had taken root. He felt a deep sense of worthlessness, a quiet, gnawing pain that made him want to withdraw completely from the world.
